Education and its role in character building

Wednesday, 22 Aug 2014

I often think of modern education system and its role in character building which lays the foundation of any great society. Being a teacher for more than 30 years I have often pondered over the end result of all the classroom teaching. Two hundred and ten working days are spent churning out piles of notebooks which are further reduced to some answer sheets of repeated information to be converted into marks/grades displaying a child’s progress unable to commensurate with life outside classroom. So many formative years are lost in the confines of classroom teaching where a child feels only short of a prisoner. Is this life meant to be wasted listening to the drab monologue of a teacher which many a times is so incomprehensible that children even go to sleep, if awake, not because they are interested in that monologue but because they think this is the only way out of school . The best time spent in school is when they are in the play ground or without a teacher, in the company of their classmates.
