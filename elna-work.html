ELNA AT WORK



Value Based Projects @ Schools

They are the flagship projects under ELNA format which are being run in various schools and are based on the (Value Based Integrated Learning)VBIL methodology. We conduct tailor made value based integrated learning projects which are implemented in schools from K- 12 in an academic year along with guidelines where teachers are given training on how to develop value based integrated lesson plans.

Steps to Implementing Value based Projects @ Schools
  • Choosing the theme for the academic year
  • Developing Activities based on the theme for students from K-12 for a period of 12-14 weeks. These activities will involve understanding the problem from a holistic view point which would involve three step format of
    • Observing/ understanding the problem through a detailed discussion in class.
    • Exploring the problem through experiments done hands on.
    • Expressing their solutions for the problem to the world outside their school environment.
  • Culmination of the Project at the end of 14 weeks


Training the Trainer

We are involved in conducting training workshops for teachers, where the focus is on how to develop inquiry and value based learning programs for children which are based on the curriculum for K-12.



Rural Urban Student Exchange program

The rural- urban student exchange program launched by ELNA in association with ACT in Delhi NCR focuses on how to bridge the difference and challenges in the rural and urban schools by promoting exchanges between them via knowledge and culture.

Objectives of the Program:
  • To help students have a holistic development provided through experiential learning academically as well as on the self development aspect.
  • To gives urban school students the opportunity to visit nearby villages and interact with their rural counterparts to share each others’ stories and understand their lifestyles, beliefs and practices.
  • To give first hand experience and a deeper insight into the rural India which forms 70% of Indian population.

The Rural Urban Student Exchange Program was successfully launched last year with The Banyan Tree World School and the Bandhwadi Government School students participating in this 4 month long program. The program was introduced from Grade 6-9 students where students from TBTWS visited the village twice and were involved in re-developing the community resource center at  Bandhwadi by providing learning aids, furniture and books for the library .

The Government school students visited The Banyan Tree World School at the end of the program and spoke about up-cycling, its importance and how this method has helped their mothers in attaining regular employment. There was also a discussion on the issues related to health and hygiene followed by a healthy interaction among the students of both schools.



Dastak- a clarion call by young minds

ELNA is an endeavor at transforming the child from a passive learner into an achiever. Dastak is one such step forward in creating value based education.

Dastak is a clarion call by young minds to awaken the society from its deep slumber seeped in dead habits and rituals of social issues such as gender bias, illiteracy etc. It’s an initiative taken by ELNA to make children empathize with the social issues and draw people’s attention to these problems to their street place enacted at a public place like Epicenter.

It’s an effort to bring together the detached section of the society by creating a common bond of understanding compassion and empathy.

Purpose:

Dastak is a process to channelize and bring children, who are going to shape the country in coming years, in the mainstream, and give them an insight into the plethora of problems plaguing the Indian society along with the ability and will to solve them.
