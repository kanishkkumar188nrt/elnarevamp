WHY ELNA?

ELNA (Experiential Learning via Nature Based Activity) is a learning format that brings young minds in direct contact with Society as a whole and the various organs governing it in particular. We firmly believe that real knowledge can not take place within the four walls of a class room. Thus interaction with civil society, private institutions, government bodies and individuals creates the much needed synergy to bring out the desired learning which is the aim of education.

School/colleges-  Their involvement is the highest in this activity since they will be spearheading this program and bring in the much needed change in the teaching- learning process by daring to step out of a defined and designed mould to a freer and natural way of learning.

Students- are the key participants in this program and as such the major beneficiaries of the whole venture. They are the key stakeholders since their future is being cast in these classrooms and they are going to actually implement the lessons learnt here in years to come and shape the future societies of the world.

Parents-not only act as facilitators to their children in this program but are partners, whose active involvement in the process of learning is of great value, as their experience plays an important role in making the children apply the knowledge they have acquired in real life. Therefore they can not afford to be mere spectators of their children’s learning from a distance. Their guidance is required at various stages of the program, to reach the desired objective.

Society i.e. Resident Welfare Associations (RWAs) – play an important role in the process as they serve as a backdrop and are the social laboratory for identifying problems and implementing solutions found in association with them. The learning process thus acquired takes children beyond text books and gives the much required fillip to bridge the gap left by the present day system which confines the students to the boundaries of the schools and deprives them of the real life experience.

Civil Society- Interaction with them helps students to get an overview of how these organizations work towards the betterment of the society and help them to understand the concepts at both micro and macro levels.

ELNA can be implemented at grassroots easily because it believes in learning from nature and imbibes the lessons from experience rather than advocating the rote method.
